# The Order of Gold

The Order of Gold is the official name of the order of knights that live in and run The Golden Circle. This organization is structured with a strict heirarchy with many different ranks, all of which ultimately answer to the Paladins of Light, the central governing body of the order. The many different circles within the order serve a miriad set of different functions both within and beyond the Golden Circle. Of the dozens of circles that exist within the order, only a few have risen to enough notoriaty that they are widely known outside the order itself.

=== "Player Knowledge"

    ## Circle of Light

    This is the presiding circle at the top of the entire order. Members of the Circle of Light are known as Paladins of Light and are both the most trusted and often most fearsome members of the order. They are tasked with the preservation of the order as a whole, and take on a miriad of tasks to that end. The Circle of Light act both as leadership for the order, as well as highly specialized field agents and envoys for the Light. Most of the members of this circle rarely travel outside of the Halo, with rare exceptions for meetings of dignitaries or great battles.

    ## Stonework Circle

    Keepers of the roadways and practitioners of infused masonry. These knights are tasked with maintaining the Halo, as well as the various spoke roads connected to it. In addition, members of the Stonework Circle are skilled masons, capable of infusing their works with magic. They are often sought after for arcane assistance in building projects.

    ## Brightpath Circle

    This is an order of mageblades, skilled magical swordsmen who are sworn to carry out the will of the Order. Known as saviors to their allies and certain doom to their enemies, these practicioners of magic and steel are practiced killers and deadly combatants alone or in a group.

    ## Twinlight Circle

    Twinlight clerics are tasked with overseeing the beginning and ending of life. These members of the order are often the most caring and personable of their ranks, as they assist those encountering one of the hardest times of life. Members of the Twinlight Circle are trained in both medical and spiritual practices around birth and death, often specializing in one of the two. The name of the circle, Twinlight, refers to the specific family of rituals and beliefs the Order of Gold share around the beginning and end of life.

    ## Caretaker Circle

    The Order has many halls and many further and varied buildings and campuses dedicated to its mission. The Caretaker circle are those who maintain all these vast properties. Whether it's ensuring the fires have fresh wood to feed them or cleaning away the debris after an unfortunate battle, Caretakers tend to the task with careful attention.

    ## Lightless Circle

    The thing about being a large publicly identifiable benevolent organization is that sometimes you need to do things to survive that simply do not fit that narrative. This is where the Lightless Circle finds its home. These members of the order are tasked with carrying out the dirty work of maintaining a small empire that the other circles cannot. Knowledge of this circle is closely guarded and unknown to most within the order, let alone outsiders. While not a rule, members of the Lightless Circle have a habit of not leaving survivors behind where they may have been seen. As such, while news of their acts may spread, the nature of the true culprits is never known.
    
    Members of this circle undertake a binding ritual that adds an additional clause to Golden Law, the Fourth Law. The Fourth Law states "Be not touched by brightness, be not bound by Light" and in effect makes those of this circle unbound by the First Law. In addition, the Fourth Law grants members of this circle an affinity for darkness unlike their bretheren. Any member of the Lightless Circle who is touched by shadow, even slightly, may fully envelope themselves within it and dissapear.

## Golden Law

All members of The Order of Gold are sworn to a strict code known as Golden Law. While not widely read, this text is publicly available and is provided to potential recruits well in advance of their joining the order. Golden Law is a practical extrapolation of the spiritual following that most of the order also practices. While faith is not a requirement of service, adherence to Golden Law is. In fact, Knights sworn to the Order are bound magically to Golden Law, and are unable to break from these conditions.

### First Law

> Bring not pain to one who would bring you comfort. Bring not kindness to one who would lay you low. Bind none who would see you free. Offer always the hand and the Light.

This first law is often interperted as a "do no harm" oath, and that is in a way true, but in practice it is a bit more complicated. The second and third sentence here create a somewhat tricky space for knights of the Order of Gold to navigte. If you're not to show kindness to your enemies, but also forbidden from binding anyone who would not themselves put you in bondage, you are in practice left with only the option of ending your enemies. The first law is an oath to do no harm, yes, but it is also an oath to take no prisoners.

### Second Law

> Your word is your breath. Break it and cease to breathe.

The second law is pretty straight forward. Knights of the Order of Gold do not lie. Their word is their breath, and they are magically bound such that once a falsehood is spoken, they will no longer be able to draw in air to breathe. Many recruits have been pruned from the early ranks of the knighthood by failing to abide this law.

### Third Law

> The Light shines through each as it does through itself. Hold none above the Light, for the Light is all.

A somewhat more complex command for followers of the Golden Law, the Third Law presents a variation on a common religious theme of monotheism. The Light, the entity which the order both worships and is sworn to by way of the Golden Law, is said to be present in all living creatures. At the same time, adherents to Golden Law are asked to keep no individual entity in higher regard than the Light itself. In combining these two concepts, we find another strong root for the benevolent nature of the Golden Circle.
