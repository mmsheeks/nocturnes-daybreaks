# The Golden Circle

The Golden Circle is home to an ancient order of knights and practitioners of magic based in the heart of [The Golden Plains](../regions/golden-plains/index.md). The sprawling compound features lush gardens, temples dedicated to the religion practiced by members of the order, libraries, training fields, a well appointed arts district, and comfortable living quarters for both members of the order and their guests. All of this is surrounded by miles of circling golden farm land that the order maintains to provide for itself and those who need the aid of the knights.

## The Halo & Surrounding Region

At the outer edge of the circular compound is a ring of roads that intersect with spoke roads that travel all the way to the center of the circle. Travelers who cross or travel along this roadway in the dark often note that it appears to have a faint golden glow, which gives it its name, The Halo. 

??? warning "SPOILERS"

    The Halo is in fact imbued with a strong spell of protection, which the [Order of Gold](../../groups/order-of-gold.md) reinforces on a regular basis through small stoneworks placed and refreshed throughout the path. This spell poses no risk to any who travel the road without ill intent for those within. One seeking entry within The Halo who bears ill intent for those protected within however will find themselves forever wandering along The Halo itself, unable to make any progress further towards the center.

Beyond The Halo these roadways continue in various degrees of repair. Several of the spokes continue well beyond the edges of the Golden Plains and act as main arteries of trade. Others dwindle to little more than a dirt walking path within a few miles of the circular road.

These roads are what connect the other farming residents of the region to the circle and the world beyond. The fields that are tended to around the edges of The Golden Circle are tended by local farming families, some of whom have been living in the area as long as the circle has been around, others having moved here more recently in search of safer and more stable climates than those to either the East or the West.

While some who live in the area bemoan the Golden Circle for taking up the best land for their showy golden fields, rather than more practical work crop, most see the presence of the order as beneficial, or neutral at worst. The order is often seen providing aid in rebuilding buildings and tools damaged in the frequent storms that roll over the fields, and if the worst comes to pass the order has been known to take in orphans or those with no where else to turn.
