# North Shelf Forest

To the Northern edge of the woodlands an expanse of dense tall trees cover much of the land as the ground rises up to join the [Crescent Ridge](../crescent-ridge-mountains/index.md). This forest is largely wild land, though the Eastern edge of the forest has seen regular incursion by various different logging parties over the past 200 years or so. While the lumber trade would certainly like to move further into the [Northern Shelf](../crescent-ridge-mountains/northern-shelf.md), the steep and unforgiving terrain makes most industrial excursions deep into this forest unwise.

The trees of this region feature ash gray bark mottled with rich rust-red. Pine like needles on wide canopies that grow high on the tree. Maturing at around 400 years and living for upwards of 1200, these trees stand anywhere from 40 to 90 feet tall.
