# The Five Teeth

Of the many peaks that make up the Crescent Ridge, the Five Teeth stand tallest and sharpest. Rising several thousand feet above their neighbors, five peaks of this range extend up into the sky at such a steep angle that even snow struggles to cling to the hard wind-worn rock faces.

=== "Player Knowledge"

    The Five Teeth are largely unexplored by those who do not otherwise call them home. Four of the peaks are located in remote and hard to access sections of [The Crescent Ridge](./index.md), making reaching them a difficult proposition, let alone setting up camp to venture up farther. One is located in the easy traversable land of [The Aketh Expanse](./aketh-expanse.md), though its nature makes exploring it a difficult and unrewarding prospect.

    While some of these peaks have been explored or even settled by remote and hard to access settlements, the majority of the world considers them simply marvels to be observed from afar. On a perfectly clear day, all five teeth are visible from the base of the central one within the Expanse. 

=== "DM Knowledge"

    The teeth are the arcane artifacts of a great working by High Sorcerer Edward Thume of the House Aketh, which lead to [Nocturne No.2](../../events/nocturnes.md#nocturne-no2). Thume was leveraging the highest available mountains of the Crescent Ridge in an attempt to create a spell engine large enough to forcibly summon [Aoukethiir](../../entities/aoukethiir.md) into the mortal world. To do so, he needed them tuned to specific frequencies. Thume reshaped these peaks, forming the tuning mechanism to cast his spell.

    While Thume's sorcery ultimately succeeded, forcing Aoukethiir into the mortal world effectively started [Nocturne No.2](../../events/nocturnes.md#nocturne-no2). The deity, being of a nature far more ancient than this world, unraveled upon entering the mortal plane. Aoukethiir's magic spread across the entirety of the world, starting from the Five Teeth, blanketing the sky in an arcane storm that lasted for 200 years. 
    
    During this storm, the teeth were struck by arcane bolts so many times that it caused the transformation of the rock surface we see today, turning the rough stone surface to a sharp glassy texture. Modern mining efforts below The Five Teeth often find evidence of the magical torment the rocks above suffered shown thousands of feet below in glassy black veins shaped like lightening that spiderweb through the mountains.

Exact details about these peaks wouldn't be known to most in world characters. There are a select few organizations who would have done the research to have rough guesses. Generally speaking, these peaks are simply discussed as being far taller than any of their peers, and largely inaccessible.

Each of them do have specific names and references in cultures of the region, so those details can be found below. If you are playing a character who would not know these details, this could be spoiler territory.

## Azimuth: The Northernmost Tooth

Located in the [Northern Shelf](./northern-shelf.md), the northernmost tooth is known by the name of Azimuth. This peak has gone largely unexplored, as the terrain surrounding it is so unfriendly to travelers that the only viable way to approach it would by by airship.

While it has yet to be confirmed, some sailors off the Northern coastline have reported seeing sheer stone well below the surface of the water on calm nights that much resembles the glassy nature of the Teeth.

If this is true, it would imply that Azimuth is both taller and reaches much deeper than any of its surrounding peers. Given the sheer cliff of the Northern Shelf, it is possible that the formation that gave rise to that imposing peak does indeed stretch below the waves.

Where the name Azimuth originates from has largely been lost to time, though the name has persisted. Current history traces its origins to one of the many black metallic stone statues erected throughout the [Aketh Expanse](./aketh-expanse.md).

??? warning "SPOILERS"
    An exploratory company from one of the Jewels of the Ridge has, indeed, flown an aircraft up to attempt to verify the height of Azimuth. While their craft could not fly high enough to crest the peak, they estimate its height at 17,500 feet.

## Kaerok: Northern Tribes Summit

Located in the range of mountains bordering the North of the [Aketh Expanse](./aketh-expanse.md), Kaerok is one of the more approachable peaks among the Five Teeth. This is not, however, due to its natural position.

The Northern Tribes of the Aketh Expanse, an isolated group of magical practitioners and carpenters, maintain a sprawling network of footbridges that climb from peak to peak towards Kaerok. At the top of this network, anchored fast into the glassy stone of the Tooth itself, is the sacred shrine these tribes call their spiritual home.

Devoted to a great spirit of thunder, Kaero, this shrine and the beliefs of these tribes also give name to this peak. Kaerok, or "Home of Kaero," is considered a sacred and protected land by those who make this area their home.

??? warning "SPOILERS"
    As a right of passage, when a priest or warlock of the Northern Tribes comes of age, they perform the Rite of Flight. During this rite, the elders of the tribes are gathered to bid Kaero forth from the summit to give their boon of storm wind to the young practitioner. The initiate then flies using their own willpower and the aid of their spirit to meet Kaero upon the peak of their home.

    Skilled warlocks and clerics of Kaero can call upon this power of flight freely while this close to the peak, and do so to fly up above the summit with some regularity. The elders of the tribes records indicate its height as being 19,000 feet, while the highest recorded flight thus far exceeds that, reaching 21,000 feet.

## Daggertip: The Central Peak

In sharp contrast to the surrounding subdued terrain of the [Aketh Expanse](./aketh-expanse.md), Daggertip rises at a nearly vertical angle up through nearly the very center of the entire Crescent Ridge mountain range.

Adventuring parties who have traveled close to the base in the Aketh Expanse say it pierces through the rolling smooth mountains like the tip of a sharp blade thrust up from beneath the soil. They report that the mountain is made of an almost glassy obsidian like material, distinctly unlike the surrounding terrain.

While some attempts to climb Daggertip have been made, most parties give up within the first few hundred feet. The slope of the mountain is unrelenting, providing very few carve outs where one can naturally rest. Between those points the ground is both too slick for most to gain foothold, and made of too dense a material to be dug into with picks to create anchors.

Given the sheer challenge the climb presents, none have made an attempt in recent years. It is only on a clear day that the tip of the mountain is visible above the clouds, but some researchers have made use of this sight along with the relatively traversable terrain at Daggertip's base to estimate the height of the peak. It is said to rise 21,200 feet above the sea, making it the tallest peak in the Crescent Ridge Mountains by a margin of over 5,000 feet.

??? warning "SPOILERS"

    At the base of Daggertip within the Aketh Expanse there is a shrine left behind by [House Aketh](../../groups/great-houses.md#house-aketh) that tells the story of the Sorcerer Thume. While most believe these shrines to have been worn away by time, in fact the only thing necessary to reveal their message is to cast them in fairie fire.

## Iiblo: Southern Jewel Peak

Iiblo, or more accurately the range surrounding it, is home to one of the [Jewels of the Ridge](./jewels-of-the-ridge.md). This mighty mining city is carved into the foothills of the Ohgway Range with deep branching tunnels of the mines that reach far below the mountains.

These mines eventually encounter the roots of Iiblo, so named by the miners who dig there. These glassy obsidian tendrils stretch down through the mountain range as deep as anyone has ever mined. The Ohgway miners have made it a generations long mission to mine and map the roots of Iiblo, having found great riches in the enchanted minerals that surround these deep and wide roots of dark stone.

While their mining efforts have ventured many hundreds of feet below sea level, and many thousands of feet up into the mountains themselves, the Ohgway have yet to find an end to the dark glassy stone of Iiblo. It seems to pervade the entire mountain range, as though the stone was pulled up through the other mountains.

??? warning "SPOILERS"

    While it was not the Ohgway who commissioned them, an exploratory company from one of the Jewels of the Ridge has flown an aircraft up to attempt to verify the height of Iiblo. While their craft could not fly high enough to crest the peak, they estimate its height at 18,700 feet.

## Glasstop: The Southernmost Tooth

Glasstop is unlike the rest of its siblings in that it does not rise to a unsummitable peak. In fact, the highest point of Glasstop is at exactly 18,000 feet where it has been cleanly cut off and made into a sheer glass obsidian plateau.

The edges of this plateau fall away at the same steep angle as its siblings, and Glasstop shares all the same characteristics as the others. What great working of magic severed the peak and let it fall is lost to history, but the peak itself can be found buried at only a slight angle in a valley far below.

Accessible only by airship, a camp of artificers and miners is set upon the plateau peak of Glasstop. This camp rotates its staff regularly to prevent oxygen starvation, and has no permanent facilities. Their goal is to work with this rare access to the core at the peak of one of the Teeth to better understand their makeup and origin.
