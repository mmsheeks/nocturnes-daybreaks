# Crescent Ridge Mountains

The Crescent Ridge Mountains span the entire eastern coastline of the continent. The range of these mountains spans about 300 miles across at its densest point, fittingly in the middle of the range geographically. As the range extends to the north and south it becomes thinner across, though it maintains much of its vertical height. While several well traveled paths through these mountains exist, one one is maintained year round while the rest are available only when the whether permits.

The foothills to the West are protected by the [Eastern Woodlands](../eastern-woodlands/index.md), making the mountains a remarkably secure backstop for the many port cities and towns that dot the Eastern coast. A small few of these coastal cities, known as the Jewels of the Ridge, are large enough to extend into the mountains proper. To the northern and southern ends of the continent the mountain terrain comes so close the water edge as to make civilized settlement impractical, these regions are known as the Northern and Southern Shelves.
