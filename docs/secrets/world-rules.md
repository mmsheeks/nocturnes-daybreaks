# Foundational World Rules

These shouldn't necessarily be stated explicitly anywhere in the world material, but the material should all adhere to these rules. If you're going to break them, there must be some extreme reason.

## 1 - Everything ends in a Nocturne.

While some things may persist through a Nocturne, nothing may pass through one unchanged. Fundamentally, all things must end when a Nocturne reaches its climax. Materials, geography, life, and knowledge may persist, but not in the same form.

## 2 - Gods are Gods. Spirits are not.

The world has deities. They may choose to present themselves in lesser forms, and are not always truthful, but a deity is a special type of thing separate from all else. Some spirits may reach towards those heights of power, but are not of the same fundamental nature as gods.

## 3 - Magic has a Cost.

Power can be derived from many places, but no source gives it freely.

## 4 - Time always moves forward.

This can't be violated, even by magic craft. A fundamental rule, not enforced by The Bookkeeper, but simply brought into being by the reality of his domain.
