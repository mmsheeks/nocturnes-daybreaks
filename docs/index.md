# Introduction

Welcome to the working copy of the lore for my homebrew world, a project I have named Nocturnes and Daybreaks. The working title might not stick, but it fits the general aesthetic I'm aiming for. While I've done my best to hide important knowledge, some things that are not yet secret might become so later, as this is very much a working copy. Reading further is at your own risk for spoilers.

First, a quick note on what this collection of documents is. This is not a final authoritative source on the world of Nocturnes and Daybreaks. Instead, think of this as a living wiki that will update and morph as the world and setting evolve. At first, this will largely be through my own work developing concepts, adding them to the collection, and refining them as I go. Eventually, I plan to add some introductory sessions in the setting that I imagine will further inform the development of the setting. All this to say, just because something is written here, or perhaps is missing, does not mean it is set in stone. Ultimately I will continue to hold that editorial discretion to myself, in session, while DMing.

## Time in the Setting

I wanted to include a note here on time. Throughout these documents you'll hear reference to "year X". These refer to time within the current known era of history. Nocturnes and Daybreaks is a project name, but they are also mechanical measures of time within the world I am creating. 

**Daybreaks** describe times of abundance, growth, and prosperity. These are periods of time in which societies can form, develop technologies and traditions, create art, and flourish. 

**Nocturnes** are times of catastrophe, be that swift, or slow. History may or may not survive a Nocturne, leading to new eras in the Daybreaks following them without knowledge of the old.

The current historical era spans from the start of [The Second Daybreak](./events/second_daybreak.md), which was roughly 1200 years ago. For sake of flexibility, I'm not going to be exactly strict on dates. Expect things to be in the correct order, and roughly paced as intended, but some things may move around a bit. The important thing here is to keep in mind the world is much older than its inhabitants can recall.

## A Quick Note on Mechanics

For now, this can be largely divorced from the mechanics of any specific system. Eventually this world will be adapted to a system, likely D&D 5.5e, at least that is my intent at time of writing. That said, developing a world takes time, and who knows what might come out between now and then that could be a better fit. For now, assume that I am writing with the intent to adapt the content to 5.5e (or 2024 D&D) mechanics.
